About Me

I'm a bilingual (English/Spanish) undergraduate student at Georgetown University pursuing a double major in Computer Science and Neurobiology, graduating in May 2025. My academic journey has been driven by a fascination with how technology can advance our understanding of the human brain.

At the DARN Lab (Disorders of Attention and Recovery in Neurology), I work on developing diagnostic web-based tasks for aphasia research and immersive VR applications for EEG experiments. This work sits at the perfect intersection of my two majors — using computational tools to study neurological conditions and brain function.

My research focuses on creating tools that require millisecond-precision timing for synchronization with EEG equipment, which presents unique challenges in web development. I've also been exploring how virtual reality can create controlled, immersive environments for neuroscience experiments, particularly using Meta Quest headsets.

Beyond research, I'm passionate about teaching and mentorship. As a Teaching Assistant for Georgetown's CS department, I've had the opportunity to guide over 150 students through programming courses in C++, Java, Python, and R. There's something deeply rewarding about watching concepts "click" for students who were previously struggling.

When I'm not coding or studying, I enjoy exploring the latest developments in neurotechnology, diving into new programming languages and frameworks, and thinking about how emerging technologies like brain-computer interfaces might shape our future.
